Family Space Sample Comparison

Side-by-side figures for the original MMS large-metro sample and the expanded top-100-c25 sample. The purpose is to see whether the graphical evidence changes when the center/periphery classification is expanded.

The visual result is stable: central family-space scarcity predicts larger parent and fertility gaps between periphery and center. The expanded sample still does not show a robust metro-wide fertility decline.

Reading

The graphs say the same thing in both samples. The robust object is not a metro-wide decline in recent-birth rates. It is the spatial incidence: where central family-sized stock is relatively scarce, parents and births are more tilted toward the periphery.
