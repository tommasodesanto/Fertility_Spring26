Family Space: Expanded Top-100 Metro Diagnostic

This is an alternate ACS/MMS diagnostic using the top 100 CBD regions by 2000 population and a looser center-PUMA rule. It is meant to test whether the family-space facts survive outside the original large-metro sample.

The expanded sample strengthens the spatial-reallocation reading. Parent centrality gaps and fertility gaps are larger where central 3+ bedroom stock is relatively scarce. Actual center fertility is lower in high scarcity metros, but metro-total fertility is not robustly lower.

Sample Definition

The default MMS sample keeps regions with 2000 population above one million and uses a strict center-PUMA cutoff of 50% core population. That produces 42 metros in the fertility-supply regressions.

This expanded sample instead sets MMS_TOP_N_REGIONS=100 and MMS_CENTER_PUMA_CORE_SHARE=0.25. The looser center cutoff is needed because PUMAs are coarse in smaller metros; otherwise many top-100 regions have no usable center PUMA.

Actual Fertility Levels

The level regressions answer the two direct questions. Center recent-birth rates are lower where central family-space scarcity is higher, but the continuous slope is not statistically significant in the expanded sample: -0.0031, SE 0.0026, p=0.242. Metro-total recent births are not lower: 0.0014, SE 0.0017, p=0.430.

Interpretation

  • The expanded sample supports the core sorting fact: parents are less central where central family-sized stock is scarce.
  • It also supports a spatial fertility-gradient fact: recent births and own children are more peripheral where central family-sized stock is scarce.
  • It does not, by itself, show lower metro-wide fertility. That needs either stronger price/supply variation or a dynamic design.
